- Treat close or grouped hostiles as an emergency: if any hostile is within 2 blocks, two or more are within 6 blocks, or you take heavy damage, immediately stop attacking or eating and sprint-kite away from the nearest hostile until you have 10–12 blocks of separation.
- Do not open with melee against groups: if a bow and arrows are available and a hostile is more than 4 blocks away with clear line of sight, shoot while backing up; otherwise retreat first to split the group before fighting.
- Use melee only when it is clearly safe: fight one nearest isolated hostile only while health is above 12, no other hostile is closing from behind, and you can maintain spacing; disengage immediately if health drops below 12 or another hostile enters close range.
- At low health, prioritize escape over all damage: below 8 health, never melee unless trapped; sprint away, place blocks between you and hostiles if you have blocks, avoid allies’ paths, and eat only after several blocks of clearance are secured.
- After danger is low, recover before moving on: eat available safe food until hunger is full or nearly full, equip the best armor, and keep the best weapon, food, and blocks accessible.
- After any pickup or inventory change, immediately run equip_best_gear or an equivalent action to equip armor, ready the best weapon, and keep food, blocks, bow, and arrows accessible if available.
- Cooperate only to reduce mob pressure: if healthy, shoot or pull one hostile off a hurt ally from the side without body-blocking; if hurt, announce low health and retreat away from allies so mobs do not cluster.
- Create or reuse custom actions for repeated survival behaviors: equip_best_gear, eat_when_safe, bow_kite_hostile, panic_retreat_and_block, and safe_melee_nearest; include health thresholds, hostile-distance checks, ally avoidance, block placement when available, and a short time limit.
- When a new wave appears, test the safer default: retreat first, use bow/kiting and blocks to split enemies, and switch to melee only after spacing is stable and one target is isolated.
